# Games As Art
The goal of a game is *to give the player something to achieve*. The player enters a game as a voluntary attempt to overcome obstacles. Each game has *rules(constraints)* - what you can't do as much as what you can.

The player makes *meaningful* choices - so they must have agency to affect outcomes of the experience.

Game is a series of interesting decisions. Choices where the right answer isn't obvious and trade offs are real.

The system has to respond to the players behavior in legible ways, so they can learn, adjust and feel the consequences of their choices.

A game needs elements of *uncertainty* - from randomness, hidden information or other player.

*Skill development* - the player can get better over time.

*Tension and resolution* - pursing a goal under constraints with uncertain outcome.

Games we can have *separate reality* where game rules apply.

The riches games balance uncertainty,skill development, tension, resolution and game own reality intentionally.


## Interactive art
The artist wants to express something.

The work isn't complete without the viewers action. Choreographic possibilities

Interactive art is space of possible images/sound/state/behaviors.
Artwork is system that runs its *rules* of responses. The artist composes relationship between person and system.

Viewer in the loop - body, attention, choices and presence become materials the artist is working with.

Coupling of input and response - participant does something and the system does something back. The quality of the response matters. The character of coupling - it'S latency, legibility, proportionality - this is where expression lies.

The artist is saying something through the system.

The interaction is there to produce an experience, provoke a thought, reveal something about the participant and the world.

Discoverability - figuring out what the work does is part of the experience. *Exploration is meaningful*

The participant is collaborator and subject - they been observed and responded to - audience turned into content.

Interaction art unfolds - pacing, anticipation, fatigue, surprise are compositional elements.

Emergence - things the creator didn't predict. 

## Commonalities Games and Interactive Art
Feedback in games informational, feedback in art expressive.

Both are systems that complete themselves through a person participation - they are finished when someone is engaging.

Range of experiences let the viewer traverse it.

Response loop - system reacts to what the participant does.

Rules as generative medium - constrains are the work, the medium is a set of behaviors.

Voluntary engagement - you can't be forced to play a game

Time as structure - both unfold - pacing rhythm, anticipation and surprise.

Both are participatory systems - authored possibility spaces that complete themselves through presence and action.

Games create possibility spaces towards challenge, mastery and resolution.

Interactive art towards meaning, reflection and experience.

Building a participatory system, how much do I want it to behave like a game versus like an artwork.
